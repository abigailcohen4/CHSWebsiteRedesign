Broadcasting
Visual Arts Digital Video Journalism Programming
TV

Communications High School has a newly renovated TV studio. In 2015, it went under a $1.5 million renovation with the Radio Studio. Students have access to high-definition Hitachi cameras, professional quality teleprompters, a new camera jib, green screen cyclorama, a redesigned lighting system, and updated audio equipment. In the TV control room, there is a Tricaster video switcher, video routing controller, point of view camera and headset-based communication system. Students have access to all of this equipment and programs to enchance their broadcasting skills and to push their creative limits. Classes are taught by Jennifer Cornine.

Students take half a marking period of an Introduction to Television, where they familarize themselves with the equipment and the different roles in the studio.

The Live Studio and Production elective challenges and prepares students to create a live news broadcast for one marking period. It is shared across CHS at 9:00am for daily news and updates.

The Advanced Studio Production elective further challenges students to explore different types of shows. They create episodes and longer segments of television.

Radio

Communications High School has a newly renovated Radio studio. In 2015, it went under a $1.5 million renovation with the TV Studio. The radio studio and the control room were renovated and include a new digital audio mixing console, microphones, and a state-of-the-art Vidigo visual radio system. There is also acoustic paneling throughout the studio. Students familiarize themselves with the equipment and learn different roles in the field. There are also Macs and Blue Mouse Microphones with the Adobe Suite on them for the students to explore. Classes taught by Bill Bengle.

Students take half a marking period of an Introduction to Radio, where they familarize themselves with the equipment and the different roles in the studio.

The Live Studio and Production elective challenges and prepares students to create a live radio broadcast for one marking period. It is shared across CHS at 9:00am for daily news and updates.

The Advanced Studio Production elective further challenges students to explore different types of shows. They create hour long radio shows and enhance their skills.
